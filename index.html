More About Me

I'm Nima Niazi, an Iranian musician and developer based in New York. I've been a professional musician for almost 13 years, performing live with numerous pop artists. I'm currently a graduate student and a coding lab mentor at ITP , an interdisciplinarity program by NYU Tisch .

I embarked on my musical journey at the age of 8 by taking piano lessons with a local music teacher. At the age of 16 I was bitten by the rock 'n roll bug, bought myself an electric guitar and have been ardently learning the ins and outs of the instrument ever since. Besides playing the instrument itself I'm fascinated by the diverse sound pallette it has.

I graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree from University of Tabriz in English Language and Literature in the year 2015. I decided to pursue my lifelong passion, music. In 2018, I joined an Iranian pop band as the lead electric guitar player and played live shows all over Iran and in neighboring countries until 2021. To further expand my skills and to find artistic and creative applications of the latest technology, I joined ITP.

- Milad Concert Hall, Tehran, Iran - Dec. 2021

Mid 2021, I launched my own music educational web application Neckboard.com. I am constantly working on making the whole experience better and much smoother by adding constant updates. I've been fascinated with programming since an early age, I started out by taking Visual Basic courses when I was around 16 years old. Then I dived deeper into web development and worked as a front-end developer. As a musician, I also enjoy audio DSP programming and have experience coding with C++ and the JUCE framework to design audio effects.
